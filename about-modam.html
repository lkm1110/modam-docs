The name

Gather.
Hold.

Modam comes from Korean words that mean to gather and to hold—to collect what moves you and keep it close. We built a space around that instinct: the quiet pleasure of curating what you love, slowly, until it becomes unmistakably yours.

Etymology

A word for the collector’s heart

모으다 · 담다 — to bring together, to gather; to contain, to hold in one’s hands or in one’s life.

Pronounced mo-dam — a soft gathering, a held space.

Collecting is never only about objects. It is about attention—what you return to, what you allow to stay. Modam exists for people who treat taste as a practice: choosing pieces that speak to them, arranging a life around beauty, and letting art feel less like a distant institution and more like part of the room you actually live in.

Everyday art

Closer than it used to be—
still not close enough

For a long time, art has often felt distant and difficult. Compared to the past, it has gradually entered more familiar parts of everyday life—through interiors, design, and the textures of how we live—but even so, it can still feel like something not easily within reach: guarded, opaque, or meant for someone else’s wall.

We believe it deserves a gentler door. Modam is a place where emerging work meets people who are ready to look closely: students and artists building their voices, and anyone who wants to gather originals and prints with the same care they bring to the rest of their taste.

A space built around the process of collecting what you love—and shaping it, piece by piece, into your own.
